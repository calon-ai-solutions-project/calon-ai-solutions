[Heading2] 2.4 How to Present Proof Ethically When You Don't Have 20 Completed Builds
The honest framing that still converts:
Do not claim 20 completed transformations when you have 3. Do claim 3 delivered systems with specificity that makes them more credible than 20 vague testimonials.

The site should use this exact language in the proof section header:

'We have completed [X] operational builds. Every system on this page is real, built, and in use today. Where we show a diagnostic finding rather than a delivered outcome, we say so.'

This is not a weakness — it is a trust signal. It tells the founder that when you claim something, it is true. Most agencies claiming '100+ transformations' on their site have nothing behind it.

[Heading2] 8.4 Diagnostic Story — HeatGlow (TIER 2: DIAGNOSED)
TRADES — HEATING & BOILER  |  HEATGLOW  |  TIER 2: DIAGNOSED
(Opportunity identified through operational diagnostic. System in scoping.)
THE DISCOVERY:
Five interconnected bottlenecks identified during discovery — in order of revenue impact:

1. Lead leakage — enquiries arriving on Friday afternoons and weekends converting to zero
2. Out-of-hours black hole — no response, no booking option, no qualification outside business hours
3. Diary waste — 2-hour booking windows for jobs taking 45 minutes; dead capacity not redeployed

All CTAs go to /diagnostic. Not to a form. Not to an email. To the calendar.

Place a CTA block after every major section on the homepage.

'Want to see this in action? [Book the Diagnostic →]'

After the proof section:
'Ready to find out what we'd find in your business? [Book the Diagnostic →]'

These don't need to be large — a single line with a button. But they must be there. Not every founder scrolls to the bottom.
[Heading2] Booking Page Conversion
Three conversion paths to the booking page.
Path 1: Calendar booking (primary) — Calendly embed, book directly.
Path 2: WhatsApp — for founders who won't book online. Link opens WhatsApp chat.
Path 3: Direct email — for founders who prefer email first.

All three must be visible on the booking page. The calendar is primary. WhatsApp and email are fallbacks that capture leads who would otherwise leave.
[Heading2] Confirmation Experience
What happens immediately after booking — currently not specified.
On booking:
1. Redirect to a confirmation page (not just a Calendly 'thank you'). Calon-branded. Message: 'Booked. You'll get a calendar invite within the hour. No prep needed.'
2. Confirmation email with: date/time, one sentence ('Think about a job that recently didn't go as planned — that's usually where we find the biggest leak'), and a direct mobile number.
3. 24 hours before the session: one reminder. Text preferred. Email if no mobile.
4. Same day as session: the one-page diagnostic delivered by email.
